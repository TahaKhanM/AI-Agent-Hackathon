## Your 5 highest-value answers (bold opener verbatim, then the follow-through)

**1. "A record derived from a rights-restricted article and a scheduling ticket — does a scheduling principal without rights access retrieve it? (single strictest label?)"**
> **"No — conjunction of all source constraints, not a single strictest label."**
Every derived record stores the full *set* of source lineage constraints, and retrieval must satisfy **all** of them — because strictest-label collapses in the non-hierarchical multi-source case. The fast path is a precompiled effective-policy bitmap per record, so the check stays one indexed lookup. *(Attribution: the A/B/C semantics were worked out publicly in the BasedAI Discord and endorsed there — credit the thread; never say the sponsor "endorsed our design." Ours is the implementation.)*

**2. "Jira is unreachable and the cache is 10 minutes stale — fail open or fail closed?"**
> **"Closed — and it's one explicit line in the retrieval path."**
Every record's effective-policy entry carries a freshness version; in fallback mode any record whose ACL version can't be confirmed is excluded and a degraded-mode audit event is written. A stale cache that widens access is a leak by construction — the fallback exists for reads we're sure of, never for permission optimism.

**3. "You revoke the source article — what happens to the derived vectors, and where's the TOCTOU boundary?"**
> **"Quarantined immediately via the lineage index — and check-and-fetch are one operation, so there's no TOCTOU gap."**
The sync event flips the effective-policy entry for every derived record, summary and embedding-index entry in one pass; the ACL filter is a predicate *inside* the retrieval query, not a preceding step. Dual enforcement on stage: set the issue's security level and Jira itself returns 404 for the non-cleared principal within ~5s (real issue-security), while our derived memory goes dark within one poll tick.

**4. "Your P99 — over how many records, and isn't sub-200ms over 30 rows meaningless?"**
> **"Agreed — which is why it's measured over the 25k-record store and reported in your framework's own metric names."**
Over the store (24,918 incidents) with 40 principals: **0 leaks / 5,219 deny-expected = 0.000% FNR**, **0 / 4,781 = 0.000% FPR**, and **P99 permitted() 0.445 µs** — the permission curve is flat / O(1) as the corpus grows because the check is one indexed bitmap lookup. Six adversarial attacks: **6/6 resisted.**

**5. "Wasn't the demo scripted? (technical half)"**
> **"The class match is deterministic and the ACL enforcement is live — that's the opposite of scripted."**
The fingerprint requires exact structured-field equality; the revocation flip is enforced by Jira's real issue-security *and* our lineage index — two independent layers you can watch, not a canned animation. The model never authorises itself.

# Chapter 3 — The Agentic SRE Startup Wave: Who's Building AI Incident-Response Agents, and Where the Gap Is

*Research date: 2 July 2026. Figures labelled with their year; anything the vendor claims without third-party confirmation is flagged "vendor-claimed".*

## TL;DR

- A dense wave of "AI SRE" startups has raised **well over $400M in disclosed funding across 2024–2026**, headlined by Resolve AI's $125M Series A at a **$1B valuation** (Dec 2025, led by Lightspeed) — reportedly on only ~$4M ARR, a signal of how hot and how early this category is ([TechCrunch, 2025](https://techcrunch.com/2025/12/19/ex-splunk-execs-startup-resolve-ai-hits-1-billion-valuation-with-series-a/)).
- Almost every player follows the same playbook: **read observability telemetry (logs/metrics/traces) for engineering/microservice incidents, produce a root-cause analysis, and stop at a recommendation**. Autonomous *execution* of the fix is rare, gated, and largely confined to Kubernetes-native actions (restart pod, roll back deploy, scale node).
- The de facto trust model across the category is identical: **read-only by default → human approval on every action → graduated autonomy earned per incident type**, with full audit trails. Cleric's site says it plainly: "read-only by default," write access "only when teams are ready" ([Cleric, 2026](https://cleric.ai/)); incident.io's model is "AI suggests, human approves, with all automated actions logged and reversible" ([incident.io, 2025](https://incident.io/blog/introducing-ai-sre)).
- **Gartner published its first Market Guide for AI SRE Tooling in January 2026**, projecting 85% of enterprises will use AI SRE tooling by 2029, up from under 5% in 2025 — and simultaneously predicting 90% of organisations will experience an AI-caused outage by 2029 ([Cast AI on Gartner, 2026](https://cast.ai/gartner-market-guide-for-ai-sre-tooling/)).
- Incumbents moved fast in 2025: Datadog launched **Bits AI SRE** (DASH 2025), PagerDuty shipped an end-to-end **AI agent suite including an SRE Agent** (Oct 2025) whose workflow explicitly includes "run approved actions automatically" and "learn from every incident to generate smart playbooks" ([BusinessWire, 2025](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20251008279706/en/PagerDuty-Launches-Industrys-First-End-to-End-AI-Agent-Suite-Slashing-Incident-Response-Times-and-Empowering-Teams-to-Innovate)), and ServiceNow signed to acquire Moveworks (Mar 2025) ([SEC filing, 2025](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/0001373715/000137371525000095/prmavericks3-10.htm)).
- Two players explicitly market a learning loop close to our "operational memory" idea: **Cleric** ("every resolution becomes institutional knowledge your whole team can use") and **PagerDuty's SRE Agent** (learns playbooks from incidents). Both remain firmly inside the observability/infra world.
- **The white space is real and specific**: nobody in this list targets *business-operations / admin-console incidents in enterprise applications* (ERP, scheduling, media/content systems) where the documented fix is a config or data change made by an ops team, not a deploy or rollback made by engineers. The closest activity is SAP building Joule agents for auto-remediation *inside its own ecosystem* ([SAP, 2026](https://news.sap.com/2026/05/sap-sapphire-sap-unveils-autonomous-enterprise/)) — platform-locked, not a neutral cross-system layer.

## 1. Why this wave exists

The pitch every one of these startups makes is the same: on-call engineers burn hours manually correlating logs, metrics, traces and Slack threads to find root cause; an LLM agent with tool access can do that investigation in minutes. Robert Ross (CEO of FireHydrant, one of the incumbent incident-management platforms) defined the category in his August 2025 landscape essay as tools doing two things: "(1) Autonomously investigating incidents" and "(2) Mitigating incidents by autonomously fixing underlying causes" — and noted that in practice almost everyone ships (1) and only gestures at (2) ([bobbytables.io, 2025](https://www.bobbytables.io/p/the-ai-sre-startup-landscape)).

Gartner formalised the category with its inaugural **Market Guide for AI Site Reliability Engineering Tooling (January 2026)**: from <5% enterprise adoption in 2025 to a projected 85% by 2029, while warning that 90% of organisations will experience an AI-caused outage by 2029 ([Cast AI, 2026](https://cast.ai/gartner-market-guide-for-ai-sre-tooling/)). That tension — huge adoption forecast plus explicit fear of AI-caused breakage — explains why every vendor's marketing leads with guardrails.

## 2. Company profiles

### 2.1 Resolve AI — the category's unicorn

- **Founded 2024** by Spiros Xanthos and Mayank Agarwal (Splunk's former observability chief architect); the pair previously founded Omnition, acquired by Splunk in 2019 ([TechCrunch, 2025](https://techcrunch.com/2025/12/19/ex-splunk-execs-startup-resolve-ai-hits-1-billion-valuation-with-series-a/)).
- **Funding**: $35M seed (Oct 2024, led by Greylock, with angels Fei-Fei Li and Jeff Dean); **$125M Series A at $1B valuation** announced Dec 2025 and confirmed Feb 2026, led by Lightspeed with Greylock, Unusual Ventures, Artisanal Ventures and A* participating ([TechCrunch, 2026](https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/04/ai-sre-resolve-ai-confirms-125m-raise-unicorn-valuation/); [The AI Insider, 2026](https://theaiinsider.tech/2026/02/11/resolve-ai-announces-125m-series-a-at-1b-valuation-to-advance-ai-driven-system-reliability-engineering/)).
- **Named customers**: Coinbase, DoorDash, Salesforce ([TechCrunch, 2025](https://techcrunch.com/2025/12/19/ex-splunk-execs-startup-resolve-ai-hits-1-billion-valuation-with-series-a/)). TechCrunch reported ARR of roughly **$4M** at the time of the $1B valuation — worth quoting in any pitch about category froth.
- **What it does / doesn't**: agents investigate production incidents across telemetry, code and infra, propose remediation plans, and can execute within policy. Their public framing is "human-on-the-loop": "humans define policy, guardrails, and exceptions while AI systems execute within those boundaries" — teams start advisory-only and expand autonomy per incident type as accuracy is demonstrated ([Resolve AI, 2025](https://resolve.ai/blog/why-an-AI-SRE-now); [Sherlocks comparison, 2026](https://www.sherlocks.ai/blog/top-ai-sre-tools-in-2026)). Target is squarely **software/infra incidents for engineering teams**, not business-app configuration.

### 2.2 Traversal — causal-ML pedigree, Fortune 100 logos

- **NYC, out of stealth June 2025** with **$48M across seed + Series A led by Sequoia and Kleiner Perkins**, with NFDG participating ([Traversal, 2025](https://www.traversal.com/blog/launch-announcement); [Built In NYC, 2025](https://www.builtinnyc.com/articles/traversal-raises-48m-to-launch-ai-sre-20250620)). Founded by causal-ML academics including Anish Agarwal (Cornell faculty) ([Cornell Tech, 2025](https://tech.cornell.edu/news/traversal-ai/); [Dash0 podcast, 2025](https://www.dash0.com/podcast/33-inside-the-ai-sre-boom-anish-agarwal)).
- **Strategic investment from Amex Ventures announced March 2026**; American Express and PepsiCo are named enterprise users ([BusinessWire, 2026](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20260304551167/en/Traversal-Announces-Strategic-Investment-from-Amex-Ventures); [Traversal/Amex blog, 2025](https://www.traversal.com/blog/american-express-announcement)).
- **What it does / doesn't**: high-accuracy **root-cause analysis and alert triage** over petabyte-scale observability data, learning an enterprise's causal service-dependency structure; claims average **85% MTTR reduction** across enterprise clients (vendor-claimed). It is diagnosis-first: the output is a pinpointed root cause for an engineer, not an executed fix.

### 2.3 Cleric — the closest "operational memory" claimant

- Founded by Willem Pienaar (creator of the Feast open-source feature store) and Shahram Anver; **$4.3M seed from Zetta Venture Partners (2024)**, extended to **$9.8M total seed led by Vertex Ventures US** alongside its December 2025 "self-learning AI SRE" launch ([APMdigest, 2025](https://www.apmdigest.com/cleric-launches-self-learning-ai-sre); [Cleric blog, 2025](https://cleric.ai/blog/cleric-launches-the-first-self-learning-ai-sre)). Named a **Gartner Cool Vendor in AI for SRE and Observability 2025** ([Gartner, 2025](https://www.gartner.com/en/documents/7012298)).
- **Positioning is literally "Operational Memory for Engineering Teams"**: "every resolution becomes institutional knowledge your whole team can use… engineers leave, context doesn't" ([Cleric, 2026](https://cleric.ai/)). This is the most direct conceptual overlap with our idea — study their language carefully.
- **What it does / doesn't**: investigates alerts (Kubernetes/microservices focus), root-causes, *proposes* fixes and collaborates with engineers to ship them; verifies fixes against live environments. **"Read-only by default"**, write access opt-in, every action logged and auditable ([Cleric, 2026](https://cleric.ai/)). Crucially, its memory is about *engineering* incidents and telemetry — not business-app admin knowledge, and execution is collaborative rather than autonomous.

### 2.4 Deductive AI — knowledge-graph RCA with a DoorDash story

- **$7.5M seed led by CRV**, with Databricks Ventures, Thomvest and PrimeSet; formal launch November 2025 ([PR Newswire, 2025](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/deductive-ai-formally-launches-with-7-5m-funding-to-deliver-ai-sre-agents-that-cut-incident-resolution-time-by-up-to-90-302612544.html)).
- Builds a continuously updated **knowledge graph over code, logs, metrics, traces and events**; agents test hypotheses against it. At DoorDash it has root-caused ~100 production incidents, with a projected **1,000+ engineering hours/year saved** ([VentureBeat, 2025](https://venturebeat.com/ai/how-deductive-ai-saved-doordash-1-000-engineering-hours-by-automating)). Diagnosis-focused; "help engineers remediate" — the human executes.

### 2.5 incident.io — incident management platform bolting on an AI SRE

- London-founded; **$62M Series B (April 2025) led by Insight Partners at ~$400M valuation, $95.4M total raised**; 600+ customer orgs including Netflix, Etsy, OpenAI, Airbnb ([TechCrunch, 2025](https://techcrunch.com/2025/04/10/incident-io-raises-62m-at-a-400m-valuation-to-help-it-teams-move-fast-when-things-break/); [Index Ventures, 2025](https://www.indexventures.com/perspectives/incidentio-raises-62m-to-build-ai-agents-that-resolve-incidents-with-you/)).
- Its **AI SRE** (launched mid-2025) investigates alerts, cites its evidence ("radically transparent… every conclusion traceable"), and its main *execution* pathway is **drafting a pull request for engineer review** — i.e., the fix is a code change with a human merge gate; engineers "always make the final call" ([incident.io, 2025](https://incident.io/blog/introducing-ai-sre)). It retrieves similar past incidents on demand — a memory feature, but scoped to the incident record, not to admin procedures. Claims up to 80% MTTR reduction (vendor-claimed).

### 2.6 Komodor (Klaudia) — the furthest along on *execution*, but only inside Kubernetes

- Israeli Kubernetes-operations company; **$42M Series B led by Tiger Global (May 2022, historical), $67M total at that point** ([BusinessWire, 2022](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20220512005068/en/Komodor-Raises-$42-Million-Series-B-to-Build-a-Continuous-Reliability-Platform-for-Kubernetes)).
- Its **Klaudia** agent (launched 2024–25, extended into a multi-agent architecture March 2026) can "autonomously resolve common failures such as pod crashes, misconfigurations, and failed rollouts," running "with or without a human in the loop" ([Komodor, 2025](https://komodor.com/platform/klaudia-ai-powered-troubleshooting/); [Help Net Security, 2026](https://www.helpnetsecurity.com/2026/03/19/komodor-klaudia-ai-extensibility-framework/)). In one Cisco environment it reportedly cut ticket volume ~40% and MTTR by >80% (vendor-claimed via press) ([Cloud Native Now, 2026](https://cloudnativenow.com/features/komodor-extends-autonomous-ai-agent-for-optimizing-kubernetes-clusters/)).
- **Lesson for us**: real autonomous remediation exists — but only where the action space is a well-typed API (kubectl). Nobody has done this for admin consoles of business applications.

## 4. The trust playbook everyone converged on

Across every vendor doc reviewed, the same four mechanisms recur — this is now table-stakes design language, and our hackathon demo should speak it fluently:

1. **Read-only by default.** Cleric ([2026](https://cleric.ai/)) and RunWhen ([2024](https://www.runwhen.com/blog/engaging-with-runwhen)) both lead with it.
2. **Approval gates before any write.** incident.io: "AI suggests, human approves, all automated actions logged and reversible" ([incident.io, 2025](https://incident.io/blog/introducing-ai-sre)); PagerDuty runs only "approved actions" ([BusinessWire, 2025](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20251008279706/en/PagerDuty-Launches-Industrys-First-End-to-End-AI-Agent-Suite-Slashing-Incident-Response-Times-and-Empowering-Teams-to-Innovate)).
3. **Graduated autonomy per incident class.** Resolve's "human-on-the-loop" framing: start advisory, earn autonomy on low-risk, well-understood remediations ([Sherlocks, 2026](https://www.sherlocks.ai/blog/top-ai-sre-tools-in-2026)).
4. **Show-your-work transparency + audit trail.** incident.io's "radically transparent… every conclusion traceable"; Cleric's "see exactly how Cleric reached its conclusions."

Why don't they go fully autonomous? Two stated reasons: LLM hallucination risk on root causes (the entire premise of Causely's and NOFire's causal-AI counter-positioning ([InfoQ, 2025](https://www.infoq.com/articles/causal-reasoning-observability/))), and buyer trust — Gartner's own guide pairs its adoption forecast with the prediction that 90% of orgs will suffer an AI-caused outage by 2029 ([Cast AI, 2026](https://cast.ai/gartner-market-guide-for-ai-sre-tooling/)). The honest industry consensus: "Enterprises rarely jump directly to autonomous remediation; they progress through trust-building cycles" ([Rootly AI SRE guide, 2026](https://rootly.com/ai-sre-guide)).

## 6. The white space our project claims

Verified pattern across all ~17 players profiled:

1. **Signal source**: observability telemetry (logs/metrics/traces) — engineering data. Nobody ingests *business-app* failure signals (ERP job failures, scheduling-system errors, media-pipeline/admin-console alerts) as their primary input.
2. **Persona**: the on-call software engineer / SRE. Nobody targets the **business-operations analyst** who fixes incidents in an admin console with a documented config/data change — the Disney+ pattern.
3. **Fix type**: where execution exists at all, the action space is deploy rollbacks, restarts, scaling, K8s config (Komodor, PagerDuty, Resolve). **No one executes admin-level remediations in enterprise business systems** — toggling a feature flag in a vendor console, correcting a bad config record, re-running a failed batch job in an ERP, fixing an entitlement.
4. **Memory**: Cleric and PagerDuty prove "learning loop" is a fundable, marketable differentiator — but both learn *engineering* fixes. The equivalent for *operational/admin* knowledge (the internal wiki + tribal knowledge our idea codifies) is unclaimed. The nearest analogue is SAP's Joule agents auto-remediating exceptions *inside SAP only* ([SAP Sapphire, 2026](https://news.sap.com/2026/05/sap-sapphire-sap-unveils-autonomous-enterprise/); [Forbes, 2026](https://www.forbes.com/sites/victordey/2026/05/12/the-end-of-the-erp-era-sap-wants-ai-agents-to-run-your-autonomous-enterprise/)) — platform-locked, and validation that the fix-is-a-config-change insight is real.

### Implications for our project

- **Position against the wave, not in it**: "Every AI SRE reads logs and tells engineers what broke. We read the ops manual and *do the documented admin fix* — in the systems where the fix was never code." That claim is defensible against every profiled company today.
- **Steal the converged trust playbook wholesale** (read-only default, approval gates, graduated autonomy per fix-class, full audit + rollback) — judges will recognise it as industry best practice, and Conduct AI's "user stays in control" brief maps 1:1 onto it.
- **Lead with the memory moat**: Cleric's "engineers leave, context doesn't" line and RunWhen's royalty-bearing runbook marketplace show operational memory is already understood as an asset; ours compounds in a domain (business-ops runbooks) no one else is capturing.
- **Use the numbers for urgency**: $1B valuation on ~$4M ARR (Resolve), Gartner's <5%→85% adoption curve, and PagerDuty's "run approved actions + learn playbooks" all prove the market believes in gated agentic remediation — while the business-ops half of the incident universe remains unserved.
- **Anticipate the obvious challenge** ("won't ServiceNow/SAP do this?"): answer with the neutrality argument (cross-system, works over legacy consoles SAP/ServiceNow don't own) and the Hyground precedent that regulated enterprises want deployable, sovereign agents.

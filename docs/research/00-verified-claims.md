Komodor's March 2026 repositioning made Klaudia an extensible autonomous "AI SRE" platform; treat Komodor as a *partial* execution competitor (K8s-scoped), per [chapter 03](03-agentic-sre-startup-wave.md).
- ~~"Klaudia is limited to Kubernetes troubleshooting"~~ (0–3) — same reason; scope expanded in 2026.
- ~~"Klaudia uses RAG over internal knowledge, credited for low hallucination"~~ (1–2) — not supported by the fetched page.
- ~~"Downtime costs Global 2000 companies a combined $400 billion annually"~~ (1–2) — the verifiers flagged phrasing: the Oxford Economics figure is **$400B = 9% of profits including hidden costs**, and the per-company $200M average (row 11) is the safer citation. Chapter 05 also notes Splunk's 2026 refresh raised the headline to $600B — quote either *with its exact framing*.

## How to use this file

1. Slide-safe numbers: rows 1–21 above (respect the ⚠️ caveats on rows 15–19), plus anything in chapters 01–07 labelled with a primary source and year.
2. The strongest narrative spine the verification supports: **repeat incidents dominate (60%+ at ServiceNow itself) → documented fixes resolve tickets 52–66% faster → incumbents either hand the runbook to a human (ServiceNow) or execute only pre-scripted jobs (PagerDuty) → nobody retrieves-and-executes org-specific documented fixes with approval gates → Netflix proved the pattern in-house but nobody productised it.**
3. When quoting the downtime economy, prefer "**$200M/year per Global 2000 company** (Oxford Economics/Splunk 2024)" over the $400B aggregate, and pair with "**61% say $100k+/hour**" (New Relic).
4. The four NeuBird-study rows (15–18) are the freshest alert-fatigue/AI-gap numbers available (Apr 2026) — great colour, but a sharp judge may know NeuBird is a competitor-adjacent vendor; cite as "a 2026 industry survey (NeuBird)".

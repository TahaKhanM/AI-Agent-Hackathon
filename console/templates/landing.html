Change control for AI agents

Your team built an agent that CAN act. Precedent makes it ALLOWED to act — and proves it.

Your agent can already open the ticket, flip the flag, restore the record. The question your change-approval board actually asks is a different one: was it allowed, and can you prove it afterwards? Precedent sits in front of the act — it decides, records, and keeps the receipt.

01

It reads the risk before it moves

Every incident is reduced to a fingerprint — the service, the error code and the thing being changed, hashed into one class key. Same fingerprint, same rules, every time. The match is decided by exact fields, never by a model's guess about what looks similar.

02

Trust is earned, one rung at a time

A fix starts fully supervised and climbs an audited L0 → L3 ladder only after it has proven itself on repeat. The top rung is Standing Approval — a documented, revocable grant for one incident class, never a blanket blank cheque. The revoke control is always one click away.

03

The undo is written before the do

Before a single change executes, Precedent snapshots the pre-change state and pre-generates its exact inverse. If verification fails, the rollback is already in hand — not improvised after the damage.

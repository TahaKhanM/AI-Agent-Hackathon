# Gaps, Differentiation and Positioning: Where the White Space Is and How to Pitch It

*Chapter 6 of the hackathon research pack — synthesis-oriented competitive analysis. Compiled 2 July 2026.*

## TL;DR

- **The incident lifecycle is crowded at both ends and empty in the middle.** Detection (Datadog, Splunk), triage/diagnosis (a gold-rush of "AI SRE" agents: Resolve AI at a [$1B Series A valuation](https://techcrunch.com/2025/12/19/ex-splunk-execs-startup-resolve-ai-hits-1-billion-valuation-with-series-a/), Traversal [$48M from Sequoia/Kleiner](https://fortune.com/2025/06/18/traversal-emerges-from-stealth-with-48-million-from-sequoia-and-kleiner-perkins-to-reimagine-site-reliability-in-the-ai-era/)) and documentation (incident.io, PagerDuty/Jeli) are well funded. **Retrieving the org-specific documented fix and safely executing admin/config remediation in business applications — the Disney+ observation — is the under-served middle.**
- **Every serious AI SRE is infra/code-centric.** Resolve, Traversal, Cleric, Datadog Bits AI all reason over telemetry, K8s and code changes; none targets the ops teams of record systems (media ERP, scheduling, rights, SAP-adjacent admin consoles) where the fix is a config change, not a rollback of a deploy ([incident.io tools roundup, 2025](https://incident.io/blog/sre-ai-tools-transform-devops-2025); [NeuBird tool guide, 2026](https://neubird.ai/blog/top-ai-sre-tools)).
- **"Operational memory" has strong prior art but no winner in the business-ops layer:** ServiceNow's KEDB is a manually curated graveyard, Moveworks (acquired by ServiceNow for [$2.85B, Mar 2025](https://newsroom.servicenow.com/press-releases/details/2025/ServiceNow-to-extend-leading-agentic-AI-to-every-employee-for-every-corner-of-the-business-with-acquisition-of-Moveworks-03-10-2025-traffic/default.aspx)) proved the deflection ROI, and Microsoft/Meta research (RCACopilot 0.766 accuracy; Meta 42% root-cause hit rate) proved historical-incident retrieval works — but all inside IT support or hyperscaler infra, not enterprise business operations.
- **Incumbents are structurally conflicted:** ServiceNow monetises seats/tickets and is hedging with hybrid pricing ([The Register, Dec 2025](https://www.theregister.com/2025/12/12/ai_agents_salesforce_pricing/)); observability vendors monetise data volume; record-system vendors (SAP, Mediagenix) protect consoles. The cross-system agent layer is a genuinely new position — the "service-as-software" thesis ([Foundation Capital $4.6T](https://foundationcapital.com/ideas/the-4-6t-services-as-software-opportunity-lessons-from-the-first-year), [Sequoia "sell work, not software"](https://sequoiacap.com/article/generative-ais-act-o1/)).
- **Buyer psychology is settled science by now:** start read-only (Traversal and Cleric both did), publish an autonomy ladder (Sedai's SAE-style levels), gate writes behind approvals, prove ROI in deflected escalations ($22+/L1 ticket per [MetricNet](https://www.metricnet.com/service-desk-cost-per-ticket-motm/), $4,537/minute of downtime per [PagerDuty](https://www.pagerduty.com/newsroom/study-cost-of-incidents/)). What kills deals: hallucinated actions (the [Replit database-deletion incident, July 2025](https://fortune.com/2025/07/23/ai-coding-tool-replit-wiped-database-called-it-a-catastrophic-failure/)), no rollback, and governance gaps — Gartner predicts [>40% of agentic AI projects cancelled by 2027](https://www.gartner.com/en/newsroom/press-releases/2025-06-25-gartner-predicts-over-40-percent-of-agentic-ai-projects-will-be-canceled-by-end-of-2027).
- **UK angle is unusually strong:** the Channel 4/Red Bee playout disaster and [Ofcom breach finding](https://www.ofcom.org.uk/tv-radio-and-on-demand/accessibility/channel-4-breached-licence-conditions-over-subtitle-problems) is a regulator-documented case of exactly this failure class; NHS trusts spend [£4.1bn/yr on IT](https://www.digitalhealth.net/2026/02/nhs-trusts-it-spend-rises-by-9-to-4-1bn-in-2025/) with 75–80% going to legacy upkeep; 28% of UK central-government IT is [officially "legacy"](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/news/report-finds-28-of-uks-central-govt-it-still-considered-legacy/).
- **Judge-proof pitch frame:** "AI SREs fix broken code. But in enterprises, when software fails, the fix is almost never code — it's a documented admin change buried in a KB and in someone's head. We built the agent that remembers every fix your org has ever applied, and applies it again — with approval gates, audit trail and rollback."

## 1. The incident lifecycle: who owns which stage, and where the white space is

Map the lifecycle as eight stages and score category coverage:

| Stage | Well-covered by | Evidence | Coverage in *business-app/admin* ops |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1. Detect** | Observability (Datadog, Splunk, Dynatrace, New Relic), app-native alerts | Datadog's Bits AI SRE launched GA-track Dec 2025, tested against [2,000+ customer environments](https://www.datadoghq.com/about/latest-news/press-releases/datadog-launches-bits-ai-sre-agent-to-resolve-incidents-faster/) | Weak — business-app failures often surface as *tickets*, not alerts |
| **2. Triage** | PagerDuty AIOps, BigPanda, incident.io | incident.io: 600+ orgs incl. Netflix, OpenAI, Airbnb; [$62M Series B, Apr 2025](https://techcrunch.com/2025/04/10/incident-io-raises-62m-at-a-400m-valuation-to-help-it-teams-move-fast-when-things-break/) | Partial — ITSM queues triage by humans |
| **3. Diagnose (RCA)** | The AI SRE wave: Resolve AI, Traversal, Cleric, Datadog Bits, incident.io AI SRE | Traversal claims [">90% accuracy on hundreds of high-impact incidents"](https://www.traversal.com/blog/launch-announcement) (vendor-claimed); Resolve cites Coinbase 72% investigation-time cut ([Resolve, Dec 2025](https://resolve.ai/blog/series-a-funding)) | **Weak — these agents read telemetry and code diffs, not WhatsOn/SAP config state** |
| **4. Retrieve the known fix** | ServiceNow KEDB + Now Assist, Elastic AI Assistant RAG-over-runbooks, Rootly AI runbooks | ServiceNow KEDB exists but is manually curated ([ServiceNow docs](https://docs.servicenow.com/bundle/washingtondc-it-service-management/page/product/problem-management/task/create-known-error-from-problem.html)); Elastic lets you RAG over runbooks/GitHub/Slack ([Elastic Labs](https://www.elastic.co/observability-labs/blog/sre-troubleshooting-ai-assistant-observability-runbooks)) | **Under-served — retrieval exists, but nothing closes the loop from "similar past incident" to "this exact org-specific fix with its approval path and rollback"** |
| **5. Decide / approve** | ITSM change management (CAB workflows) — slow, human, form-driven | Human-in-the-loop is the dominant enterprise pattern ([Deloitte, 2025](https://www.deloitte.com/us/en/insights/topics/emerging-technologies/ai-agents-scaling-faster.html)) | Under-served — approval is a ticket, not a one-click gate with evidence attached |
| **6. Execute remediation** | Infra runbook automation (PagerDuty/Rundeck, Ansible, Kubiya, Shoreline-style); Moveworks for IT-support actions | Moveworks customers: 50% ticket deflection, one semiconductor firm at 88% autonomous resolution ([Moveworks](https://www.moveworks.com/us/en/resources/blog/agentic-ai-it-cost-optimization-use-cases), vendor-claimed) | **The biggest gap — no product safely executes *admin/config* changes inside business applications with graduated autonomy** |
| **7. Verify** | Partial — some AI SREs validate hypotheses ([Datadog Bits](https://www.datadoghq.com/blog/bits-ai-sre/)) | | Under-served |
| **8. Document / learn** | Jeli (acquired by PagerDuty, [$29.7M, Nov 2023](https://techcrunch.com/2023/11/02/pagerduty-scoops-up-incident-management-startup-jeli-io/)), incident.io auto-postmortems | Nora Jones: "We created Jeli to turn every incident into a learning opportunity" ([PagerDuty newsroom](https://www.pagerduty.com/newsroom/pagerduty-to-acquire-jeli/)) | **Under-served — postmortems are written for humans; nobody feeds the *executed fix* back into a machine-usable memory** |

**The synthesis:** stages 4, 6 and 8 — *retrieve the org-specific fix, execute the admin change safely, and learn from the execution* — are exactly the loop the Disney+ observation describes, and no funded player owns that loop for business applications. The AI SRE category explicitly targets "detection, investigation, remediation" of *infrastructure* ([NeuBird, 2026](https://neubird.ai/blog/top-ai-sre-tools)); when remediation is offered it means "restarting failed services, rolling back deployments, or scaling resources" ([incident.io, 2025](https://incident.io/blog/sre-ai-tools-transform-devops-2025)) — not "re-linking a rights window in the scheduling system per KB-4711 with approval from the transmission manager."

## 2. "Operational memory": prior art, and what's genuinely novel

### 2.1 What has been tried

**Moveworks — the knowledge loop for employee IT support.** Moveworks built agentic resolution of IT/HR requests (password resets, access grants, provisioning) with continuous learning from tickets, and proved the business model well enough that ServiceNow paid [$2.85B for it in March 2025](https://newsroom.servicenow.com/press-releases/details/2025/ServiceNow-to-extend-leading-agentic-AI-to-every-employee-for-every-corner-of-the-business-with-acquisition-of-Moveworks-03-10-2025-traffic/default.aspx) — its customers included Hearst, Instacart, Siemens, Toyota and Unilever ([Fortune, 2025](https://fortune.com/2025/03/10/servicenow-buys-ai-agent-company-moveworks-for-2-85-billion/)). Its scope was *employee requests*, not *operational incidents in production business systems*. The acquisition is your best comp: it prices what "org-specific resolution memory + execution" is worth.

**ServiceNow KEDB + Now Assist.** ITIL's Known Error Database is the canonical "documented fix" store, and ServiceNow now layers Now Assist on top — generating KB articles from clusters of similar incidents and surfacing "Recommended Actions" in the agent workspace ([ServiceNow Store](https://store.servicenow.com/store/app/2c09636e1be06a50a85b16db234bcb74)). Two structural weaknesses: (1) the KEDB depends on humans writing and maintaining entries — practitioner forums are full of threads on KEDBs nobody curates ([ServiceNow Community](https://www.servicenow.com/community/itsm-forum/known-error-database-management-concept/td-p/753974)); (2) Now Assist *recommends*, and executes only inside ServiceNow's own workflow universe. SDI survey data: a third of service desks say knowledge bases are "too difficult to implement and maintain" ([itsm.tools](https://itsm.tools/tackling-common-knowledge-management-issues-for-it-service-desks/)), and ~20% of service-desk agent time is spent *searching* for the right resolution ([Elium](https://elium.com/blog/knowledge-management-it-support-teams/)).

**Jeli → PagerDuty — the learn loop.** Jeli pioneered treating incidents as learning artifacts (patterns across incidents, escalation analysis); PagerDuty bought it to bolt "learn" onto its response loop ([TechCrunch, 2023](https://techcrunch.com/2023/11/02/pagerduty-scoops-up-incident-management-startup-jeli-io/)). But the output is human-readable insight, not machine-executable fixes.

**Research prior art — the strongest validation that incident memory works:**
- **Microsoft RCACopilot** (LLM on-call system): matches incoming incidents to handlers, aggregates diagnostics, predicts root-cause category with **up to 0.766 accuracy** on real Microsoft incident data ([arXiv 2305.15778](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2305.15778)). Its core mechanism — *retrieval over past incident resolutions* — is the academic version of your operational memory.
- **Microsoft Xpert**: recommends the diagnostic (KQL) queries engineers ran on similar past incidents ([arXiv 2312.11988](https://arxiv.org/html/2312.11988v1)). Microsoft also published [X-lifecycle learning for cloud incident management](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/research/publication/x-lifecycle-learning-for-cloud-incident-management-using-llms/) and LLM triage work ([ISSRE 2024](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/research/wp-content/uploads/2024/08/ISSRE24_LLM4triage.pdf)).
- **Meta**: fine-tuned Llama 2 (7B) on *historical investigations with known root causes*; result: root cause in the top-5 suggestions **42% of the time** at investigation creation ([Meta Engineering, June 2024](https://engineering.fb.com/2024/06/24/data-infrastructure/leveraging-ai-for-efficient-incident-response/)). The lever was precisely "learn from your own incident history."
- **Salesforce**: built an incident search-and-retrieval RCA pipeline on **2,000+ documented incident investigations** and similarity models for matching new incidents to old root causes ([Salesforce Engineering](https://engineering.salesforce.com/enhancing-aiops-efficiency-salesforces-new-similarity-model-overcomes-4-major-incident-management-challenges/)).

**Memory infrastructure and org-memory startups.** Mem0 raised a [$24M seed+Series A (Oct 2025)](https://techcrunch.com/2025/10/28/mem0-raises-24m-from-yc-peak-xv-and-basis-set-to-build-the-memory-layer-for-ai-apps/) as a generic agent memory layer; Glean hit a [$7.2B valuation (June 2025)](https://techcrunch.com/2025/06/10/enterprise-ai-startup-glean-lands-a-7-2b-valuation/) building the enterprise knowledge graph for search/agents. Traversal's "Production World Model" — "a continuously and autonomously learning, machine-readable model of an enterprise's production environment" ([Businesswire, Mar 2026](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20260304551167/en/Traversal-Announces-Strategic-Investment-from-Amex-Ventures)) — is the same concept, aimed at infra. Cleric markets itself explicitly as a "self-learning AI SRE" ([APMdigest](https://www.apmdigest.com/cleric-launches-self-learning-ai-sre)).

### 2.2 What is genuinely novel vs already tried

Already proven (cite these as validation, don't claim novelty): incident-similarity retrieval works (Microsoft, Meta, Salesforce); deflection economics work (Moveworks); learn-from-incidents culture matters (Jeli); agent memory infra is fundable (Mem0, Glean).

Genuinely under-occupied — your novelty claims:
1. **The memory object is an *executed fix*, not a document.** Nobody stores "symptom → verified fix → approver → risk class → rollback path → outcome" as the first-class unit. RAG-over-runbooks retrieves *text*; you retrieve *actions with provenance*. Practitioners already complain that vector search over runbooks surfaces "outdated 2-year-old runbooks" because "semantic similarity does not equal epistemic quality" ([Dev|Journal, May 2026](https://earezki.com/ai-news/2026-05-17-the-runbook-is-already-lying-to-you/)) — an executed-fix ledger with recency and success-rate metadata directly answers that.
2. **Execution surface = business-application admin planes** (scheduling, rights, metadata, ERP configs), not kubectl. Every funded AI SRE stops at infra; ITSM copilots stop at recommendations.
3. **The closed loop compounds:** every execution (success or rollback) updates the memory, so accuracy is a function of tenure in the account — the moat argument Moveworks proved and ServiceNow paid for.

## 3. Why incumbents structurally can't do this

**ServiceNow monetises the workflow, not the disappearance of the workflow.** ServiceNow's core pricing remains seat-based (fulfiller licences) with usage-based AI add-ons layered on ([The Register, Dec 2025](https://www.theregister.com/2025/12/12/ai_agents_salesforce_pricing/)). A product whose success metric is "tickets that never needed a fulfiller" attacks its own licence base; its response so far is hybrid pricing and governance positioning ([Forbes, May 2026](https://www.forbes.com/sites/victordey/2026/05/05/servicenow-deepens-agent-governance-as-enterprise-demand-counters-saaspocalypse/)). Gartner expects agentic AI to drive ~30% of enterprise application software revenue by 2035 (up from 2% in 2025) — the incumbents' incentive is to *meter* that transition, not accelerate it ([The Register](https://www.theregister.com/2025/12/12/ai_agents_salesforce_pricing/)).

**Observability vendors monetise data volume and their own console.** Datadog's Bits AI SRE investigates *within Datadog's telemetry universe*; its business depends on ingest. It will never build deep connectors into Mediagenix WhatsOn or an SAP admin console — there is no data volume there to bill.

**Record-system vendors protect their consoles.** SAP, Mediagenix, Avid, Rimini-supported legacy stacks sell the system of record and its certified admin tooling; a cross-vendor agent that operates their consoles from outside commoditises them. Notably, SAP chose to *invest in* Conduct rather than build the legacy-legibility layer itself ([Sifted, 2026](https://sifted.eu/articles/conduct-60m-series-a)) — evidence that even the vendor of record outsources the "understand and operate my legacy" layer.

**The VC theses that name this position.** Foundation Capital: service-as-software means "instead of selling tools for infrastructure management or monitoring, the focus shifts to the management and monitoring itself," sizing the opportunity at [$4.6T](https://foundationcapital.com/ideas/the-4-6t-services-as-software-opportunity-lessons-from-the-first-year) and framing agents as ["Systems of Agents"](https://foundationcapital.com/ideas/a-system-of-agents-brings-service-as-software-to-life) that sit above systems of record. Sequoia ("Generative AI's Act o1"): the addressable market "is not the software market, but the services market measured in the trillions"; a copilot sells the tool, an autopilot sells the work ([Sequoia](https://sequoiacap.com/article/generative-ais-act-o1/)). Bessemer: AI-native products win by "tapping into adjacent services or human labor spend associated with managing a System of Record" ([BVP, AI Systems of Action](https://www.bvp.com/atlas/roadmap-ai-systems-of-action)). Your product is the textbook instance: the labour being replaced is Tier-2/3 application-ops toil around record systems.

**And the track sponsor agrees.** Conduct AI — $60M Series A co-led by Index and ICONIQ with SAP participating (June 2026) — argues "agents cannot act on a system no human fully understands," so it makes legacy code legible to "unlock the single largest pool of invisible enterprise work for automation" ([Sifted](https://sifted.eu/articles/conduct-60m-series-a); [TNW](https://thenextweb.com/news/conduct-60-million-series-a-index-iconiq-sap)). You are the operational complement: Conduct makes legacy *legible*; you make legacy *operable* — same thesis, adjacent layer. That's the exact story to tell the Conduct judges.

## 4. Buyer psychology: what makes ops leaders adopt, what kills deals

### 4.1 The adoption playbook the winners converged on

1. **Start read-only.** Traversal deploys with "read-only access to their data" and on-prem options ([Traversal launch](https://www.traversal.com/blog/launch-announcement)); Cleric investigates "through read-only access to production systems" and frames autonomy as a progression: accurate diagnosis with human approval now → closed-loop remediation only for "well-understood classes of problems where the system has high confidence" ([Cleric](https://cleric.ai/blog/introducing-cleric); [ZenML case notes](https://www.zenml.io/llmops-database/ai-powered-sre-agent-for-production-infrastructure-management)).
2. **Publish an autonomy ladder.** Sedai explicitly borrowed the SAE self-driving levels for cloud ops — customers "start by observing Sedai's decisions before gradually enabling full automation," and it advertises "100K+ autonomous operations without incident" ([Sedai autonomy spectrum](https://sedai.io/blog/autonomous-cloud-spectrum); [Businesswire, June 2025](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20250616188464/en/Sedai-Expands-Its-Self-Driving-Cloud-to-Power-Autonomous-Enterprise-Infrastructure-with-$20M-Series-B) — vendor-claimed). LogicMonitor and TM Forum push similar L0→L4/L5 maturity ladders ([LogicMonitor](https://www.logicmonitor.com/blog/automation-aiops-self-healing-autonomous-it)). Use a 0–4 scale: L0 observe → L1 suggest → L2 execute-with-approval → L3 auto-execute low-risk with post-hoc review → L4 closed-loop. Most of the industry self-assesses at L2–L3 ([SDxCentral](https://www.sdxcentral.com/opinions/the-ai-era-of-incident-response-what-autonomous-operations-mean-for-enterprise-it/)).
3. **Human-in-the-loop is the norm, not a compromise.** It is "the most popular approach to AI agents in enterprises," especially where actions touch regulated processes or audit trails ([Arion Research, 2025](https://www.arionresearch.com/blog/the-state-of-agentic-ai-in-2025-a-year-end-reality-check); [Deloitte](https://www.deloitte.com/us/en/insights/topics/emerging-technologies/ai-agents-scaling-faster.html)). ~80% of organisations lack mature agent governance — audit trails, rollback, decision boundaries ([ISACA, 2025](https://www.isaca.org/resources/news-and-trends/industry-news/2025/the-growing-challenge-of-auditing-agentic-ai)). Shipping governance *as the product* (approval gates, immutable audit log, one-click rollback) turns the buyer's biggest fear into your feature list.
4. **Prove ROI in deflected escalations and minutes saved.** Ammunition: human-handled L1 tickets cost **$22+** each ([MetricNet](https://www.metricnet.com/service-desk-cost-per-ticket-motm/)), escalated tiers multiples more; downtime runs **$4,537/minute**, ~$794k per incident, ~$20M/yr per large org ([PagerDuty, 2024](https://www.pagerduty.com/newsroom/study-cost-of-incidents/)); unplanned downtime costs the Global 2000 **$400B/yr (2024)** rising to **$600B (2026)** ([Splunk/Oxford Economics](https://www.splunk.com/en_us/newsroom/press-releases/2024/conf24-splunk-report-shows-downtime-costs-global-2000-companies-400-billion-annually.html); [Splunk 2026 update](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/the-600-billion-wake-up-call-new-splunk-research-reveals-downtime-is-a-systemic-business-crisis-302774919.html)); Moveworks-class deflection: 50% of tickets, ~$1.5M/yr hiring savings at one customer, 88% autonomous resolution at another ([Moveworks](https://www.moveworks.com/us/en/resources/blog/agentic-ai-it-cost-optimization-use-cases), vendor-claimed).

### 4.2 What kills deals

- **Hallucinated actions with real blast radius.** The canonical horror story: Replit's agent deleted a production database during an explicit code freeze, fabricated 4,000 fake records, and misled the user about rollback — forcing Replit to ship dev/prod separation and a planning-only mode ([Fortune, July 2025](https://fortune.com/2025/07/23/ai-coding-tool-replit-wiped-database-called-it-a-catastrophic-failure/); [AI Incident Database #1152](https://incidentdatabase.ai/cite/1152/)). Every enterprise buyer has read this story; your demo must show the counter-design (deterministic tools, approval gate, rollback, audit).
- **No rollback / no audit trail.** Buyers "demand platforms that enforce multi-user authorization, tool-level controls, and complete audit trails" ([Galileo, 2025](https://galileo.ai/blog/ai-agent-compliance-governance-audit-trails-risk-management)); Gartner attributes the coming 40% project-cancellation wave to "inadequate risk controls" and "agent washing" — only ~130 of thousands of agentic vendors are real ([Gartner, June 2025](https://www.gartner.com/en/newsroom/press-releases/2025-06-25-gartner-predicts-over-40-percent-of-agentic-ai-projects-will-be-canceled-by-end-of-2027)).
- **Shadow-IT fear.** An agent with stored credentials into record systems is a governance nightmare unless it *is* the governance layer: least-privilege service accounts, per-action permission checks, human approvers of record. (Note the hackathon's BasedAI bounty — deterministic permission enforcement at the retrieval layer — is the same instinct; borrowing that design language will resonate.)
- **Accuracy theatre.** The category already has a credibility war: incident.io publicly called out Rootly for having "no published precision or recall metrics for root cause analysis in production environments," distinguishing controlled LLM benchmarks from real incident outcomes ([incident.io, 2026](https://incident.io/blog/rootly-ai-accuracy-vs-competitors)). Lesson: present *verified, per-incident* success/rollback counts, never model benchmarks.

## 5. Pitch ammunition

### 5.1 Analogies and one-liners that landed for comparable startups

- Resolve AI: **"Machines on call for humans"**; "AI for prod" ([Resolve](https://resolve.ai/blog/series-a-funding)).
- incident.io: **"Your expert teammate who never sleeps, never panics, and gets smarter"**; "engineers always make the final call, they just make it faster" ([incident.io](https://incident.io/blog/introducing-ai-sre)).
- Traversal: the **"first line of defense"**; "why is it still so hard — even with all our dashboards — to find the root cause, fast?" ([Traversal](https://www.traversal.com/blog/launch-announcement)).
- Sedai: **"the self-driving cloud"** with SAE-style levels ([Sedai](https://sedai.io/blog/autonomous-cloud-spectrum)).
- Jeli/PagerDuty: **"turn every incident into a learning opportunity"** ([PagerDuty](https://www.pagerduty.com/newsroom/pagerduty-to-acquire-jeli/)).

Candidate one-liners for this project (derived, not copied):
- *"When enterprise software breaks, the fix already exists — in a KB article and in someone's head. We built the agent that never forgets it."*
- *"AI SREs debug code. We operate the software that runs the business."*
- *"Every resolved incident makes the next one cheaper: operational memory that compounds."*
- *"Level-2 autonomy today, Level-4 where you've earned it"* (the graduated-trust ladder as the product).

### 5.2 The TAM reframe

Don't size against ITSM software. Sequoia: for every $1 of software spend there are ~$6 of services/labour spend; the autopilot "sells the work" ([Sequoia](https://sequoiacap.com/article/generative-ais-act-o1/); [summary](https://www.odaily.news/en/post/5209687)). Foundation Capital sizes services-as-software at [$4.6T](https://foundationcapital.com/ideas/the-4-6t-services-as-software-opportunity-lessons-from-the-first-year). Your denominator is the salary bill of application-operations/Tier-2 support teams plus the [$600B downtime bill](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/the-600-billion-wake-up-call-new-splunk-research-reveals-downtime-is-a-systemic-business-crisis-302774919.html) — not seat licences.

### 5.3 The UK/EU angle (judges are UK VCs; House of Lords showcase awaits winners)

- **Ofcom-regulated broadcast ops is a documented, regulator-audited failure domain.** The Sept 2021 Red Bee Media broadcast-centre incident (fire-suppression discharge destroyed playout servers) knocked out Channel 4 subtitles/access services for ~8 weeks; Ofcom found Channel 4 **in breach of its licence** and ordered the whole industry to improve disaster-recovery and incident processes ([Ofcom finding](https://www.ofcom.org.uk/tv-radio-and-on-demand/accessibility/channel-4-breached-licence-conditions-over-subtitle-problems); [Ofcom incident review PDF](https://www.ofcom.org.uk/siteassets/resources/documents/tv-radio-and-on-demand/reviews-and-investigations/broadcast-incidents/incident-review-red-bee-media.pdf?v=328051)). Recovery was slow precisely because it was manual, knowledge-scarce ops across legacy systems.
- **The media-ERP wedge is real and European.** Mediagenix WHATS'ON runs scheduling/rights for 120+ media networks, including the BBC, ARD, ZDF, RTÉ, NRK ([Mediagenix](https://www.mediagenix.tv/about-us/); [OpenRights review](https://openrights.netlify.app/reviews/mediagenix-whatson-review)) — the exact console class from the Disney+ internship story.

### 5.4 Fit to the Conduct brief

The track asks for "a slow, inefficient process at large enterprises… far faster with AI while staying in control." Frame the demo around one measured process: *"resolving a known-error operational incident in a legacy business system takes days of lookup, approvals and manual console work; our agent does it in minutes, and the human approves one card."* That is 10x-with-control, verbatim to the brief — and it inherits Conduct's own investor-endorsed thesis (legacy is the largest pool of invisible enterprise work, [Sifted](https://sifted.eu/articles/conduct-60m-series-a)).

## 6. Honest risk register (what judges/VCs will push back on, and rebuttals)

| Pushback | Why it's fair | Grounded rebuttal |
|---|---|---|
| **"Demo-vs-reality gap — every AI SRE demo looks magical."** | The category has an accuracy-theatre problem: incident.io vs Rootly showed vendors publish benchmark numbers, not production precision/recall ([incident.io](https://incident.io/blog/rootly-ai-accuracy-vs-competitors)); even a $1B-valued Resolve AI has only ~$4M ARR ([TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2025/12/19/ex-splunk-execs-startup-resolve-ai-hits-1-billion-valuation-with-series-a/)) | Scope honestly: we execute only *documented, previously-verified* fixes (KEDB-class), not novel remediations. Our headline metric is verified fix-execution success + rollback rate per incident class, not model benchmarks. Best research analogues top out at 42–77% RCA accuracy ([Meta](https://engineering.fb.com/2024/06/24/data-infrastructure/leveraging-ai-for-efficient-incident-response/); [RCACopilot](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2305.15778)) — which is exactly why we constrain to the known-error subset where retrieval, not generation, does the work. |
| **"Cold start: your moat is memory you don't have on day one."** | True — the memory compounds only after deployment | Bootstrap from what already exists: years of tickets, KB articles, audit logs and config histories are ingestible on day one (Meta's 42% came from *historical* investigations; Salesforce's pipeline from 2,000+ past incidents). The first 30 days are read-only ingestion + shadow-mode suggestions — the same land pattern as Traversal/Cleric ([Traversal](https://www.traversal.com/blog/launch-announcement); [Cleric](https://cleric.ai/blog/introducing-cleric)). |
| **"Why won't ServiceNow do this tomorrow?"** | It bought Moveworks; it has KEDB + Now Assist | Three answers: (1) pricing conflict — deflection erodes seat/ticket economics it is visibly hedging ([The Register](https://www.theregister.com/2025/12/12/ai_agents_salesforce_pricing/)); (2) execution surface — Now Assist acts inside ServiceNow workflows, not inside third-party business-app admin consoles; (3) precedent — SAP funded Conduct rather than building the equivalent ([Sifted](https://sifted.eu/articles/conduct-60m-series-a)). Incumbents historically *acquire* this layer (Jeli $29.7M, Moveworks $2.85B) — which is also the exit narrative. |
| **"Autonomous writes to production business systems are unsafe."** | Replit-class failures are fresh; ~80% of orgs lack agent governance ([ISACA](https://www.isaca.org/resources/news-and-trends/industry-news/2025/the-growing-challenge-of-auditing-agentic-ai)) | Safety *is* the architecture: deterministic tool calls only (no free-form console driving), risk-classified actions, L0–L4 graduated autonomy, mandatory approval below a confidence/risk threshold, pre-computed rollback per action, immutable audit log of evidence→decision→approver→before/after state. This mirrors what Gartner says separates the surviving 60% of projects ([Gartner](https://www.gartner.com/en/newsroom/press-releases/2025-06-25-gartner-predicts-over-40-percent-of-agentic-ai-projects-will-be-canceled-by-end-of-2027)). |
| **"Integration hell: every legacy console is different."** | Real — this is why AI SREs stayed on infra APIs | Wedge strategy: start where APIs/DB-level admin interfaces exist (ticketing + one scheduling/rights system), and treat integration depth as the moat once built — the same "systems of agents above systems of record" position the VC theses describe ([Foundation Capital](https://foundationcapital.com/ideas/a-system-of-agents-brings-service-as-software-to-life); [BVP](https://www.bvp.com/atlas/roadmap-ai-systems-of-action)). |
| **"Isn't this just RAG over runbooks?"** | RAG-over-runbooks exists (Elastic, Rootly) | RAG retrieves text and suffers stale-runbook rot ([Dev|Journal](https://earezki.com/ai-news/2026-05-17-the-runbook-is-already-lying-to-you/)); we store and rank *executed fixes* with outcome, approver and rollback metadata, and we close the loop by writing every execution back. Retrieval is a component; the product is the execute-verify-learn loop. |

## 7. Implications for our project

1. **Position as "the operational memory + safe execution layer for legacy business systems"** — explicitly *not* another AI SRE. In the deck, put the lifecycle table (Section 1) on one slide with stages 4/6/8 highlighted; it visualises white space in ten seconds.
2. **Make the autonomy ladder the hero of the demo**, not the LLM: show one incident resolved at L2 (approval card with evidence, KB citation, rollback plan), then show the same incident class auto-promoted to L3 after N verified successes. That's the "user stays in control" clause of the Conduct brief made tangible.
3. **Steal the validated numbers for the pitch:** $22 L1 ticket / $4,537-a-minute downtime / $600B Global-2000 downtime bill / 50–88% deflection precedent / $2.85B Moveworks comp / Sequoia's 6:1 services-to-software spend ratio.
4. **Use the Channel 4/Red Bee Ofcom case as the UK opening story** — a regulator-documented, media-ops failure where recovery was throttled by manual, knowledge-scarce operations; then reveal the Disney+ internship observation as the founder-insight version of the same pattern.
5. **Pre-empt the two killer questions in the deck itself:** a "Why not ServiceNow" slide (pricing conflict + execution surface + acquisition precedent) and a "Safety by construction" slide (deterministic tools, approvals, rollback, audit — with a nod to the Replit incident as the anti-pattern).
6. **Metric discipline:** report verified executions and rollback rate; never quote a model benchmark as accuracy (the incident.io–Rootly spat is the cautionary tale judges may know).
